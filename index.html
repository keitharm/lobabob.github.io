Hi. I'm Farhankhan Daya.

I am a student at UT Austin that is currently pursuing a Bachelors Degree in Computer Science. I love learning about and experimenting with new things, especially if they involve programming.

I'm Farhankhan Daya - student by day and tinkerer by night. You know how when you're a kid, you want to be everything? That's kinda how I am with computer science.


Over the past several years I've experimented with web development, created tracking software with Python, hacked applications together with C and Java, and gotten down and dirty with some x86 Assembly and multi-threading.


Exposing myself to such a wide range of development has given me the funny ability to quickly adapt to and learn new technologies and techniques.

But building applications is just the half of it. I have also spent some time literally hacking applications (for educational purposes of course) - web-based and otherwise - while in the local computer security organization at UT Austin (The Communications Society or ComSoc for short).


We've explored a variety of exploits and vulnerabilities including (but definitely not limited to) Buffer Overflows, SQL Injections, Cross Site Scripting (XSS), and my favorite, MITM (Man in the Middle) Attacks. Learning about this stuff is great because you really get to understand how these applications work. Most people see a login form and are glad when it magically works. Me? I want to know how everything behind the scenes is ticking....
